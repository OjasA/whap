"That’s unique coming from a farm boy like you. Most of your kind are only looking for war."
"I don’t see a whole lot of war. I just see some paintings and books from the passing caravans in the mountains."
"Heh, you’re as stupid as you look. Those paintings are nothing compared to the sublime Islamic art."
"Say what you like, but I do not care for your nonsense."
"Get back in the carriage, we’re going to enlighten you in the glorious teachings of Islam."

They bound your burning wrists together again with the rope and drag you off to another place. After a long uncomfortable ride, you noticed that the soldiers took you to some form of a school building. You go in and see children of various ages and races that seem to be in the same situation as you. You are told to wait in line until someone acknowledges you.

"Name?"

"Skills?"
"I do not really have one skill per se, however, I am quite adept at studying the fine arts. I love immersing myself into paintings and observing their deeper, truer meaning."
"Good for you, but do you have any actual, useful skills…?"
"Art is a useful skill, but if you must need a practical skill, I have studied much in the art of war and the masterpieces that successful strategies employ."
"OK, we’re going to make you a tactician, because even though you sometimes spend your time doing mostly useless things, you should be able to pick it up kind of quickly."
"I am already a masterful tactician, I don’t need your studies."
"Whatever, you obnoxious farmboy, just go to the class I assign you and follow the instructions, and then we shall have no problems in believing that you truly are a ‘masterful tactician’."
"Can I go home after that?"
"No."

Two entire years of rigorous classes and training pass by.
