You order your sailors to head for the chain on the Golden Horn to expose the blockaded Byzantine fleet. Your fleet of ships slowly make their way across the Bosphorus towards the defensive chain. Knowing that your naval forces could suffer heavy casualties, you surround your utility craft with offensive vessels to distract the Byzantine defensive forces in the channel while your specialists cut the chains.

As your fleet advances, the naval battle intensifies. Your largest galleys position themselves to block the enemy from seeing your chain-cutter sailors at work. The small rowing boats reach the boom, and their sailors begin to slowly cut the chain.

BOOM! An intense push by the Byzantine defense sinks many of the Ottoman galleys, exposing the defenseless chain cutters in their small rowboats. Seeing the massive loss of ships, you call the operation off, but not before suffering heavy casualties of galleys, rowboats, and more importantly, your men.

"Retreat! We cannot get past the chains fast enough, if we stay here, we are sure to die!"

Losing morale, your ships retreat back to the Ottoman shore. Knowing that the loss of a naval presence would allow the Byzantine forces to focus on the Ottoman regiments on land.

The Byzantine forces attack with immense gusto after their small victory at sea. They cause heavy casualties to the Ottoman army. Mehmed II calls for the tactical retreat of the land forces before losing more men.

The Byzantine Emperor offers a peace treaty to call off the war. Seeing as how the casualties of the Ottoman army far surpass what was expect and increasing with every passing day, Mehmed decides to accept the peace treaty. This brings an end to the Ottoman siege of Constantinople and the prosperous city continues to stand.

You have failed to take Constantinople, resulting in a crushing blow to the morale and strength of the Ottoman military.You are one of the major people blamed for the loss of the war and you a stripped of the title as a commander by Mehmed II himself. You go back into the army working as a regular footsoldier and your once boastful and revered dignity and pride disappears.
